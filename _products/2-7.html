About OpenROV

The OpenROV v2.7 is the best ROV we've ever built.

FEATURES:

  • Live HD video

    It streams HD video back to a surface laptop over an ultra-thin two-wire tether.

  • 3 thrusters

    It maneuvers using the same thruster configuration as previous models, however new hydrodynamic adjustments, new motors, and new motor speed controllers have resulted in greatly improved handling and control, especially in the vertical direction.

  • LED lights and Lasers

    A new LED board provides even more illumination in the depths, and the lasers provide both scaling and distance measurement capability.

  • Live HD video up to 1080p with wide-angle lens and tilt function
  • LED lighting for low-light environments
  • Easy-to-use web browser user interface
  • Beaglebone Black AND Arduino MEGA microprocessors for a flexible and powerful developer platform with dozens of input/output channels and plenty of computing power for user-designed features and experiments
  • Rechargeable lithium batteries (sold separately)
  • 100-meter lightweight 2-wire tether
  • Compact and lightweight--backpack or carry-on ready
  • Payload area for additional hardware or equipment
  • Join our open source, ocean-loving community--find all the software and hardware files online as well as a vibrant and enthusiastic community to share adventures, ask questions, and meet other people with passion for exploration
  • All open source hardware and software designed for end-users to change
  • Over the Internet software and firmware updates

Physical specifications:

  • Weight 2.6kg
  • Dimensions 30cm long x 20cm wide x 15cm high
  • Nominal battery life 2-3 hours (depending on use)

Performance specifications:

  • Maximum depth 75m (246ft)
  • Maximum tether length 300m (100m tether provided)
  • Maximum forward speed 2 knots
  • LED brightness 180lm
  • Temperature capability -10C - 50C
  • Software controlled camera tilt (+/- 60 deg from center)

Instrumentation:

  • HD Webcam (120 deg FOV) with audio
  • Red Scaling lasers (parallel, 10 cm separation)
  • Numerous current and voltage sensors
  • External I2C bus with 3.3V power for external instrumentation
  • 6 additional auxiliary wires for user-defined external instrumentation or devices

Minimum system requirements

  • Chromebook or laptop from within the last 3 years with RJ-45 ethernet connector
  • OS/X/Windows/Linux
  • Free USB port
  • Latest Chrome browser

Community additions include controlling ROVs using mobile phones, Firefox and Safari browsers, as well as using wifi connections to the topside adapter.
