Trident Drone Is the Perfect Boat Accessory

It lives on the boat with it’s case and a laptop. We have a TV on the boat and we can play with it whenever we want to see what’s down there,” Mark said. His motor yacht is stored in San Diego and his OpenROV is as much part of his boat as any accessory. “It’s mostly for fun” but also the drone has more than paid for itself. When Mark was bringing the boat down from Canada, he used the drone to check the depth as he was coming into a harbor because “the water looked much shallower... You usually just follow the chart, which said 70 feet.” But OpenROV was able dive down and using the depth sensor report back that it was much shallower than his draft. “So if I didn't have the drone I would have run aground and been stranded.” Another time when Mark was in Seattle the drone came to the rescue again. “I hit a crab pot in Seattle and I could see that the line was cut but I could still feel a slight shudder. The drone was a lifesaver. If I didn't check the prop and see that there was a steel cable still around it I would have blown a seal and flooded the engine room.

Trident’s sharp video and WiFi capabilities make it an excellent tool and toy for boat owners. Because it’s small and rugged you can take it onboard and have eyes underwater in seconds. Special thanks to Mark from Arizona for this amazing and cool set of stories.

Is an Underwater Robot Your Next Dive Tool?

While my dive table still has a place in my gear bag, many divers have switched to dive computers. Divers are always looking to expand their capabilities, go deeper, and dive smarter. Many OpenROV users are also divers (some even start diving after seeing what’s down there with their ROV) and some have found interesting ways to use OpenROV on your next dive.

Trident aims to be a great tool for both recreational and commercial divers. Kirby, one of our community members, spends a good deal of time looking for anomalies to dive.

I’m not interested in jumping overboard 20 times a day...when I can just put Trident in the water.

Kirby

Another ROV’er, Doug, runs a dive charter boat and plans on using Trident to help maximize the dive time by doing a site survey with Trident before suiting up to for a deep dive. Doug also said,

I can keep an eye on all the divers in the water with Trident...with high vis I could run the ROV with the divers and keep them monitored.

Doug

Often times there are friends and family on the boat who don’t dive and still want to participate. Trident gives them the chance to keep up with the divers. And since Trident is small and can dive to 100m, it will often be able to go where it’s too dangerous

Underwater Research Using Drones

Water covers over 70% of our planet and we know more about the surface of Mars than we do about our oceans and deepest lakes

From a converted bedroom filled with computer displays, Charles Cross, an OpenROV software engineer, piloted the Trident down through the crystalline waters of Lake Tahoe. Within minutes the researchers could see the Tahoe as it emerged from the blue gloom on the lake floor...The 154-ton, 170- foot-long steamer was once called the "e;Queen of the Lake,"e; carrying passengers and mail in style. Built in San Francisco in 1896, it was intentionally scuttled in 1940.

Now the hobbyist team hopes it can democratize the science and adventure that has previously been accessible only to someone with the resources of Robert Ballard, the oceanographer and explorer who has investigated ships like the Titanic and the Bismarck, a German battleship sunk in the early stages of World War II.
