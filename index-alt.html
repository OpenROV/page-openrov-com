OpenROV Trident Shark Encounter

In October 2017 we sent one of our Trident Underwater Drone beta units to dive with Great White Sharks in Isla Guadalupe. Every year, these sharks aggregate at this remote island off the coast of Mexico to feed on tuna and elephant seals.

Scientists are studying the behavior and population of the sharks. By using acoustic tags, satellite transmitters, and photo ID, they help to protect this shark hotspot. The use of underwater drones offers a new, safer, and less intrusive way to study the sharks and give insight into their behaviour without the direct presence of humans.

FAST & MANEUVERABLE

Trident can quickly scan large areas, but also maneuvers precisely in close quarters.

With speeds up to 2m/s, up to 4h run time and a maximum depth of 100m, Trident sets new standards for this class of ROVs.

EASY TO USE

Use your phone or tablet to control the drone and stream the video.

You can stream full HD video straight to your device from deep underwater. Excellent color rendention, great low-light performance, and industry-leading latency make Trident the vehicle of choice for capturing quality video.

DURABLE & ULTRA PORTABLE

Trident is compact enough to be carried in a backpack or tucked under an airplane seat and is tough enough to earn its keep in any professional setting.

AN EVOLUTIONARY LEAP IN CONNECTIVITY

Trident uses a wireless radio to communicate with the pilot’s phone, laptop, or tablet, which greatly increases Trident's effective range. A Kevlar-reinforced and neutrally-buoyant tether makes handling and retrieving Trident easy.
